2021 Great Britain Road Safety

Situation

The UK Government's Department of Transport publishes reports on Road Safety on an annual basis. These reports are supported by the release of datasets containing the information related to:

  1. Road accident details in the calendar year
  2. Vehicles (involved in road accidents)
  3. Casualties (related to road accidents)
  4. test

Task

Supplied with the 2021 data files, the task was to conduct exploratory data analysis on the information contained in these files and to compare the data analysed to the official 2021 annual report.

Important headline metrics to be reviewed were:

  1. Road fatalities and KSI
  2. Casualties by severity and month
  3. Vulnerable road users
  4. Fatalities by type of vehicle
  5. Casualties by age and sex

Actions

The data files were processed in Kaggle to:

  1. load the accident, vehicle and casualty files;
  2. read the Safety Open Dataset Data Guide to obtain the descriptions for codes in the data files;
  3. replace the codes in the data files with the descriptions contained in the Data Guide;
  4. review any missing (null) data in the data files;
  5. calculate the KSI (Killed and Severity) measures using the available adjustment probabilities (published in separate data files for accidents and casualties since 2004);
  6. extract the headline statistics in tabular and graphical format and compare to the annual report.

Feel free to view my work in my Kaggle notebook - Great Britain Road Safety Exploratory Data Analysis

Results and Findings

  • The datafiles had very little missing data - with the all missing data being in the accident location co-ordinate fields.
  • The accident and casualty severity data was supplied unadjusted. To support comparison to the annual report's headline KSI metrics (which use adjusted Severity measures) adjustments were made using the methodology advocated by the Department of Transport.
  • The analysis uncovered that whilst the data files contains a number of categorised/grouped data fields, that data features had to be re-categorised to support direct comparison with the UK Govt reporting (for example: casualty age bands).

A summary of the comparison of this analysis and the UK Govt headlines follows:

  1. Road Fatalities and KSI

    The analysis supported the headline value of 1,558 fatalities (1.2%) of 128,209 total casualties in 2021.
    Furthermore, using the adjusted casualty severity, the KSI (Killed and Seriously Injured) count is 27,450 - representing 21.41% of all reported casualties in 2021. These align perfectly with the UK Govt headline reporting.

  2. Casualties by severity and month

    The UK Govt reported the following analysis for 2021:

    1. In the period January to March 2021, the third national lockdown in England, the number of casualties decreased considerably.
    2. Fatalities reached their lowest point since the start of the COVID-19 pandemic in February 2021.
    3. KSIs and casualties of all severities followed broadly similar patterns to fatalities in 2021, reducing during the period of lockdown, and reaching their highest levels during the second half of the year.
    4. Casualties also showed a small downward trend between November and December 2021, whilst there were high levels of Omicron COVID-19 variant transmission and a reduction in road traffic.
    The below graphs, showing the Fatalities and KSI metric over the 2021 calendar year, supports these observations.

  3. Vulnerable road users

    The UK Govt reported the following analysis for 2021:

    1. Car occupant casualties accounted for 44% of road fatalities and 55% of all road casualties in 2021.
    2. In 2021, 55% of casualties were car occupants, 13% were pedestrians, 12% were motorcyclists and 13% were pedal cyclists.
    The below graphs confirm the UK Govt findings regarding vulnerable road users of road fatalities and all road casualties.

  4. Fatalities by type of vehicle

    The UK Govt reported the following analysis for 2021:

    • Most fatalities occur in collisions involving a car.
    The below graph agrees with the the UK Govt fatality numbers and the finding that most fatalities occur in collisions involving a car.

  5. Casualties by age and sex

    The UK Govt reported the following analysis for 2021:

    1. Overall, 78% of fatalities and 62% of casualties of all severities were male.
    2. 3% of fatalities and 10% of all casualties were aged 16 and under.
    3. 26% of fatalities and 30% of all casualties were aged 17 to 29.
    4. 19% of fatalities and 6% of all casualties were aged over 70 and over.
